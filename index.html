  → Why?

Why not?

I'm fascinated by doors, espeically in other countries, where the lives being lived on the other side of the doors is likely to be very different than my own. This page features doors I loved enough to photograph in China and India.

I also adore elephants. I was lucky enough to get to see keepers bathing their elephant charges up close at Kappukadu Elephant Rehabilitation Centre while I was in India. There was definite love between the keepers and the elephants, and the joy that the elephants clearly felt at playing in the Neyyar river wasa wonderful to see.

And as for puns, they make the world a better place. Or at least a punnier place.

  → Give me an opening:

When life closes a door...Open it back up; sort of how doors work.
I used to work at a revolving door company. Then I thought, "this job is going nowhere fast."
Knowing how to pick locks has really opened a lot of doors for me.
A clown held a door open for me the other day. I thought, “what a nice jester”.
When is a door not a door? When it’s ajar.
What do you call a cute door? Adoorable!

  → A large undertaking:

What do you get if you cross an elephant and a fish? Swimming Trunks!
An elephant is drinking out of a river when he spots a turtle asleep on a log. The elephant ambles over and kicks the unsuspecting turtle clear across the river.
“Why did you do that?” asks a passing giraffe.
“Because I recognized it as the same turtle that took a nip out of my trunk 47 years ago.”
“Wow, what a memory!” says the giraffe.
“Yes,” says the elephant. “Turtle recall."
What do you call an elephant covered in mud? DIRTY!
How do you get an elephant out of the water? Wet.
How do you get two elephants out of the water? One by one.
What do you call an elephant that doesn’t matter? An irrelephant!
